# Scientific and creative boundaries

SONIFICATION is a mathematical composition and procedural-synthesis laboratory.
It maps explicitly declared mathematical structures to musical controls and
audio. The output may reasonably be described as **audible geometry** when the
mapping and provenance are supplied.

That phrase does not turn a composition into a physical measurement.

The project does not claim that:

- E8, Phi, qutrit logic, an attractor, or another mapped structure is physically
  present in an audio file or in nature;
- a procedural render validates a physical theory;
- a deterministic classical algorithm performs a quantum computation;
- a local fingerprint proves authorship, copyright ownership, or uniqueness
  against an external catalogue;
- equal hashes imply anything beyond exact equality within the declared byte
  domain.

Future integrations with QEC data must preserve the distinction between an
input dataset, a classical model, a sonic mapping, and a physical experiment.
The manifest records the mapping contract so those layers remain inspectable.
